## 1. Repo Overview

This is a self-described "workflow-oriented, tool-neutral" SOP starter designed to impose an AI-assisted engineering discipline on any project, regardless of stack. Its central thesis is a five-step lifecycle: plan → task-spec → implement → validate → write-back-or-promote. The repo consists almost entirely of *meta-documentation* — rules, guides, templates, and example facts — rather than application code. It explicitly avoids binding to any particular framework, language, or toolchain. The canonical entrypoint is `.references/ai-engineering-sop/AGENTS.md`, with `.references/ai-engineering-sop/CLAUDE.md` serving as a redirect shim. A `.references/ai-engineering-sop/CLAUDE.md`/`.references/ai-engineering-sop/AGENTS.md` split is the adapter model for dual-tool environments. Skills live under `ai/skill/`, guides and templates under `ai/doc/`, and always-apply behavioural rules under `.cursor/rules/`. The stated design goals — lightweight enough to copy, technology-agnostic, keeps rules/templates/facts/skills separate — are evident throughout. Its highest-density portable artefact is `.references/ai-engineering-sop/ai/skill/plan-to-spec.md`, which provides the nine-step plan-to-spec workflow plus a twelve-item common-failure-modes checklist. The repo is self-aware about when to *not* use its own structures: the plan template, the phase-aware guide, and the project-startup SOP all carry explicit gates discouraging heavyweight adoption for simple tasks.
