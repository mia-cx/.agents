# awesome-cursorrules — role-to-sop audit 1

Source: `.plans/audits/awesome-cursorrules/raw-findings.md` (operational observations only; every substantive claim below maps to that file).

---

## 1. Repo overview

The audited material describes **awesome-cursorrules**, a curated registry of third-party Cursor rule files: a root **`.cursorrules`** aimed at maintainers of the list, plus **`rules-new/*.mdc`** shards (YAML-frontmatter Cursor rules) and hundreds of **`rules/<slug>/.cursorrules`** entries. The corpus mixes **language-agnostic engineering playbooks** (clean code, Gitflow, documentation, tickets), **agent-conduct meta-rules** (verify before claiming, edit pacing, tone bans), and **deep stack SOPs** (Next.js App Router, Medusa, Convex, Cypress, mobile, blockchain, JVM, etc.). Many entries are **thin templates** (“proper X” bullets, fake JS `const` arrays) while others are **long product or domain briefs** misfiled under generic folder names. The raw notes repeatedly flag **path mismatches**, **truncated or corrupt upstream files**, and **missing paths** in the local mirror, so portability judgments are conditional on checkout completeness. Cursor-specific concerns include **`globs` / `alwaysApply` scoping**, optional stripping of YAML frontmatter for human-only SOPs, and **JSON-shaped** `.cursorrules` that assume tooling accepts structured rule trees. Cross-cutting **test-volume heuristics** (for example 3–5 tests per file) and **data-testid / intercept** patterns appear in multiple Cypress-related findings. Overall the registry is best treated as a **sample library** for mining clauses, not as a consistent “gold standard” policy set.
