## Context

We have received an acquisition offer of $5M for 100% of Vesta. The buyer is a mid-size music technology holding company looking to consolidate tools in the producer ecosystem. The offer values Vesta at approximately 3x our current ARR (~$1.67M ARR).

The founding team holds 72% equity (across 3 founders). The remaining 28% is split between early employees (12% vested option pool) and angel investors (16%). An acquisition at $5M would yield approximately $3.6M to founders pre-tax, $600K to the employee pool, and $800K to angels.

We are not in financial distress — current runway is 14 months at current burn rate ($95K/mo). Growth is steady at 12% MoM for the last 6 months, but we have not yet achieved profitability.

## Decision Required

Should we accept, counter, or decline the $5M acquisition offer? If countering, what terms and valuation should we target?

## Constraints

- **Timeline**: The buyer has given a 45-day exclusivity window for due diligence. LOI expires April 30, 2026.
- **Fiduciary duty**: Angels invested at a $2M pre-money valuation. A $5M exit returns ~2.5x for them — acceptable but not exciting.
- **Team**: 3 founders + 8 employees. Buyer has indicated they want to retain all founders for 2-year earnout. Employee retention terms TBD.
- **Emotional**: Founders are split. One wants to sell (burnout, wants liquidity). One wants to keep building. One is on the fence.
- **Market timing**: Music tech M&A multiples have compressed from 5-7x ARR in 2023 to 3-4x in 2026. But Vesta's growth rate (12% MoM) is above median.
- **Earnout risk**: Buyer's proposed earnout structure is $3.5M upfront + $1.5M over 2 years tied to retention + growth targets (20% YoY revenue growth).

## Options Considered

1. **Accept at $5M** — Take the deal as-is. Founders get liquidity, team gets retained. Risk: earnout terms may be aggressive.
2. **Counter at $7-8M** — Argue growth rate justifies 4-5x multiple. Risk: buyer walks, and we've burned a bird-in-hand.
3. **Counter on structure** — Accept $5M headline but negotiate: more upfront ($4M+$1M earnout), relaxed earnout triggers, accelerated vesting for employees, founder release after 12 months instead of 24.
4. **Decline and raise** — Use the offer as social proof to raise a Series A at $15-20M valuation. Risk: fundraising market is tight, and 14 months of runway isn't forever.
5. **Decline and bootstrap to profitability** — We're 8-10 months from breakeven at current trajectory. Retain full ownership. Risk: opportunity cost of saying no to guaranteed $5M.

## Success Criteria

- **If selling**: Founders each clear $1M+ post-tax. All employees are retained with fair terms. Earnout is achievable (not a poison pill). Vesta's product vision is preserved.
- **If not selling**: Path to profitability is within runway. Next 6 months show acceleration (15%+ MoM). Optionality preserved for a larger exit or sustained independence.
- **Either way**: The decision is made with clear eyes — not from fear (running out of money) or greed (holding out for a unicorn).

## Additional Context

- Comparable exits in music tech (2024-2026): BandLab acquired Cakewalk (~$3M, ~4x ARR), Plugin Alliance was acquired for an undisclosed amount (estimated 3-4x), Amuse was acquired by Downtown Music for ~$15M (~3.5x ARR, but at much larger scale).
- Our churn rate is 4.2% monthly — higher than ideal. Improving retention could significantly change the valuation story.
- The buyer's portfolio includes 3 other music production tools. There may be cross-sell synergies they're pricing in that we're not capturing independently.
- One founder's vesting cliff completes in 4 months. Selling before that would trigger accelerated vesting per our operating agreement.
- An acquisition would trigger change-of-control clauses in two of our vendor contracts (cloud hosting, sample licensing API) that need renegotiation.
